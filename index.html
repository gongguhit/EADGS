Abstract

3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) has emerged as a powerful representation for novel view synthesis, yet its reliance on accurate Structure-from-Motion (SfM) initialization makes it vulnerable to severe motion blur. Existing deblurring methods face a fundamental chicken-and-egg dilemma: accurate camera poses are needed to deblur images, but deblurred images are needed to estimate poses. Event cameras, with their microsecond-level temporal resolution, offer blur-free observations that can break this cycle, yet prior event-aided approaches rely on indirect multi-stage initialization and treat camera poses as fixed constants. We propose EADGS, a unified framework that leverages event-based initialization and learnable pose refinement for motion-deblurred 3D Gaussian Splatting. Our method consists of three stages: (1) event data preprocessing via Contrast Maximization with a novel Event Contrast Normalization to produce Motion-Aligned Event Descriptors; (2) a feed-forward vision transformer that directly predicts camera poses and dense 3D point clouds from event descriptors, completely bypassing blur-corrupted RGB-based SfM; and (3) a deblurring 3DGS training loop with Lie-algebraic pose parameterization and alternating optimization for joint trajectory-geometry refinement. Extensive experiments on synthetic (DeepVoxels, NeRF Synthetic), real scene (Tanks & Temples), and real-world collected (DAVIS346 on UAV) datasets demonstrate that EADGS achieves state-of-the-art performance in novel view synthesis quality under challenging motion blur conditions.

Method Overview

EADGS introduces a three-stage pipeline for event-aided motion deblurring in 3D Gaussian Splatting. First, raw event streams are preprocessed via Contrast Maximization to produce sharp Image of Warped Events (IWE) and dense optical flow, bridged to the RGB domain through Event Contrast Normalization (ECN). Second, a feed-forward vision transformer (VGGT) predicts camera poses and dense 3D point maps directly from these Motion-Aligned Event Descriptors (MAED), entirely bypassing blur-corrupted SfM. Third, a deblurring 3DGS training loop with Lie-algebraic pose parameterization synthesizes blur by averaging subframe renderings along interpolated exposure trajectories, enabling joint optimization of Gaussian geometry and camera poses via alternating optimization.


Deblurring Training Loop

During training, EADGS maintains a learnable pose state for each frame consisting of a central pose on SE(3) and a screw motion velocity on se(3). Subframe poses are interpolated along the exposure interval, and the rendered subframes are averaged to synthesize the observed blur. Alternating optimization iteratively switches between optimizing Gaussian parameters (with frozen poses) and optimizing pose states (with frozen Gaussians), using Lie-algebraic perturbation with manifold retraction for stable gradient-based pose refinement.
