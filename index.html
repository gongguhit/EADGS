Abstract

3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) methods have revolutionized novel view synthesis with the real-time rendering capabilities. However, these methods rely heavily on Structure-from-Motion (SfM) to obtain accurate camera poses, which fails catastrophically when input images suffer from severe motion blur. Existing deblurring 3DGS methods attempt to jointly optimize camera poses and scene geometry but face a ``chicken-and-egg'' dilemma: promising deblurring relies on accurate camera pose while accurate pose depends on clean images. Recent event-aided approaches offer a solution but do not fully exploit the geometric information embedded in event streams, limiting reconstruction quality. In this paper, we propose EADGS, a framework that exploits event-based geometry cues for robust 3DGS reconstruction under severe motion blur. We first extract geometry cues from event streams through a feed-forward vision transformer to directly infer camera poses and point clouds, completely bypassing blur-corrupted RGB-based SfM. Building upon these event-derived geometric priors, we further develop a differentiable trajectory optimization module that jointly refines camera poses with 3D Gaussians in an alternating manner. Extensive experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate that EADGS significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods, achieving superior rendering quality and accurate trajectory estimation even in scenarios with severe blurs.

Method Overview

EADGS introduces a three-stage pipeline for event-aided motion deblurring in 3D Gaussian Splatting. First, raw event streams are preprocessed via Contrast Maximization to produce sharp Image of Warped Events (IWE) and dense optical flow, bridged to the RGB domain through Event Contrast Normalization (ECN). Second, a feed-forward vision transformer (VGGT) predicts camera poses and dense 3D point maps directly from these Motion-Aligned Event Descriptors (MAED), entirely bypassing blur-corrupted SfM. Third, a deblurring 3DGS training loop with Lie-algebraic pose parameterization synthesizes blur by averaging subframe renderings along interpolated exposure trajectories, enabling joint optimization of Gaussian geometry and camera poses via alternating optimization.


Deblurring Training Loop

During training, EADGS maintains a learnable pose state for each frame consisting of a central pose on SE(3) and a screw motion velocity on se(3). Subframe poses are interpolated along the exposure interval, and the rendered subframes are averaged to synthesize the observed blur. Alternating optimization iteratively switches between optimizing Gaussian parameters (with frozen poses) and optimizing pose states (with frozen Gaussians), using Lie-algebraic perturbation with manifold retraction for stable gradient-based pose refinement.
